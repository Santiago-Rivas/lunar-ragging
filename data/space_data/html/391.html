Jimmy Wales is an American internet entrepreneur best known for co-founding Wikipedia, the collaboratively edited online encyclopedia. Born in 1966 in New Jersey, he began his career in finance and technology before launching Wikimedia Foundation in 2003, which hosts Wikipedia and related projects. Wales has played a significant role in shaping open knowledge and online collaboration, becoming a prominent figure in the digital revolution.

Early life and education

Wales was born and raised in Hawthorne, New Jersey. He developed an early interest in computers and information technology. He earned a bachelor's degree in finance from Auburn University in Alabama and later completed a master's degree in finance from the University of Alabama. During his academic years, he was involved in computer programming and internet development, which influenced his future endeavors.

Career

After completing his education, Wales worked in finance and as an options trader. In 2001, he became involved in online ventures and, inspired to improve the accessibility of knowledge, co-founded Wikipedia in 2001 with Larry Sanger. The project rapidly grew in popularity, and in 2003, Wales established the Wikimedia Foundation to support the site. Under his leadership, Wikipedia became one of the world's most visited websites, transforming the landscape of information sharing and digital knowledge bases.

Notable works and projects

Wales is mainly renowned for Wikipedia, a free, multilingual, collaborative encyclopedia. The success of Wikipedia led to the development of numerous other Wikimedia projects, including Wikimedia Commons, Wiktionary, and Wikidata. He has also been involved in advocacy for free knowledge, net neutrality, and open licensing. Wales has authored articles and spoken extensively about internet governance, open access, and the importance of community-driven content.

Personal life

Wales is known for maintaining a modest lifestyle and a focus on philanthropy. He is married and has children. Wales is interested in technology, education, and global development. He has also been involved in various charitable initiatives, supporting education and digital literacy projects worldwide. His personal interests include reading, technology, and promoting open culture.

Awards and honours

Wales has received numerous recognitions, including being named in Time magazine’s list of the 100 most influential people and receiving awards for his contributions to free knowledge and internet development. He was awarded the Renberg Prize for Innovation in the Public Interest and has been honored for his role in transforming online information accessibility.

Legacy and impact

Jimmy Wales is widely credited with revolutionizing access to knowledge through the creation of Wikipedia. His work has empowered millions worldwide to contribute to and access information freely, fostering a culture of collaboration and openness. His advocacy for free knowledge has influenced policies and initiatives around the globe, shaping the future of digital education and open access movements.
