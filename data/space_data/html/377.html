Dr. Lisa Su is an American engineer and business executive, known for her leadership as the President and Chief Executive Officer of Advanced Micro Devices (AMD). Recognized for her pivotal role in revitalizing AMD, she has been influential in the semiconductor industry, driving innovation in high-performance computing and graphics technologies. Under her management, AMD has achieved significant market share gains and technological breakthroughs, establishing the company as a leading competitor against rivals such as Intel and NVIDIA.

Early life and education

Lisa Su was born in Kailua, Hawaii, and grew up in a family that valued education and technology. She earned her bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), where she graduated as valedictorian. She continued her studies at MIT’s prestigious Lincoln Laboratory, earning a master's degree and a Ph.D. in electrical engineering. Her academic background laid a strong foundation for her future career in semiconductor research and leadership.

Career

Su began her professional journey in the technology sector in the early 1990s, working at various companies including IBM and Freescale Semiconductor. She gained extensive experience in chip design, development, and management. In 2012, she joined AMD as Chief Operating Officer and was appointed President in 2014. Her leadership coincided with AMD's strategic shift toward high-growth markets like gaming, data centers, and artificial intelligence. In 2019, she was named AMD’s CEO, becoming the first woman to head a major semiconductor company, and has since overseen a period of robust growth and product innovation.

Notable works and projects

Lisa Su has overseen the development of AMD’s high-performance Ryzen processors, EPYC server chips, and Radeon graphics cards. These products have received critical acclaim and significantly improved AMD’s competitive position. She also led the company's transition to 7nm manufacturing technology, enabling faster, more energy-efficient chips. Her strategic focus on innovation and technological excellence has earned her numerous industry awards and recognition.

Personal life

Lisa Su is known for her dedication to her profession while maintaining a private personal life. She is married and has children. She is also a passionate advocate for promoting women in STEM fields and industry leadership. Outside of work, she enjoys reading, hiking, and giving back to the community through mentorship and educational initiatives.

Awards and honours

Su has received multiple awards, including Fortune’s Businessperson of the Year (2020), the Wall Street Journal’s Innovator of the Year (2021), and the IEEE Robert N. Noyce Award (2022). Her leadership has been widely recognized for transforming AMD into a top-tier technology company and for her contributions to engineering and business innovation.

Legacy and impact

Lisa Su’s impact extends beyond AMD, inspiring women and minorities in engineering and leadership. Her success demonstrates the importance of innovation, strategic vision, and resilience in the competitive tech industry. She is seen as one of the most influential figures in semiconductors today, shaping the future of computing technology and industry diversity initiatives.
