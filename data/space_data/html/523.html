Sebastião Ribeiro Salgado Júnior, born on 8 February 1944 in Aimorés, Brazil, is a renowned Brazilian social documentary photographer known for his powerful black-and-white images that highlight social issues, environmental concerns, and the human condition. His work has earned international acclaim for its depth, artistry, and commitment to social justice. Over a career spanning five decades, Salgado has documented the plight of displaced peoples, laborers, and natural landscapes around the world, aiming to raise awareness and inspire positive change.

Early life and education

Salgado was born in a small town in Minas Gerais, Brazil. Initially, he pursued studies in economics at the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro, graduating in 1968. During his university years, he became interested in photography, initially as a hobby. In the early 1970s, he decided to dedicate himself fully to photography, inspired by a desire to depict the realities of the world's oppressed and marginalized populations.

Career

Salgado’s professional photography career began in the 1970s when he started working with environmental and social issues. His work with magazines and international organizations brought him to various regions facing economic hardship, conflict, and displacement. His notable projects include the "Workers" series, which depicts manual laborers across the globe, and the "Migration" series, exploring human displacement caused by economic or environmental factors. In recent years, his "Genesis" project has focused on pristine natural environments and indigenous cultures, emphasizing conservation and sustainability. Salgado's images are characterized by their stark contrasts, texture, and profound narratives that combine art and activism.

Notable works and projects

Salgado’s major works include the "Workers" series (1993), which captures manual labor and industrial workers; "Migrations" (2000), documenting displaced populations worldwide; and "Genesis" (2017), a photographic exploration of unspoiled nature and indigenous communities. His photographs have been exhibited globally, and his books have sold millions, raising awareness about social and ecological issues. Salgado co-founded the Instituto Terra in Brazil, a reforestation project aiming to restore degraded land and promote environmental education. His multidisciplinary approach combines photography, documentary filmmaking, and environmental activism.

Personal life

Salgado is married to Lélia Wanick Salgado, who has played a significant role in his projects and the founding of the Instituto Terra. They live in Brazil and spend time traveling for photography projects. Known for his dedication and humility, Salgado maintains a focus on social impact rather than fame. He is also a practicing environmentalist and philanthropist, committed to sustainable development and conservation efforts.

Awards and honours

Salgado has received numerous awards, including the International Center of Photography Infinity Award, the Prince of Asturias Award for Communication and Humanities, and the Ordem do Mérito Cultural from Brazil. His work has been exhibited in major museums worldwide, and he has been honored with honorary doctorates for his contributions to art and society. His influence extends beyond photography into environmental activism and social advocacy.

Legacy and impact

As a pioneer of social documentary photography, Salgado’s images have influenced generations of photographers and activists. His compelling visual storytelling has raised awareness about global issues such as poverty, forced migration, and environmental degradation. The integration of art and activism in his work demonstrates the power of photography as a tool for social change. His environmental initiatives, notably the reforestation of degraded land in Brazil, exemplify his commitment to sustainability and ecological stewardship.
