Mark Alan Ruffalo (born November 22, 1967) is an American actor, producer, and activist known for his versatile performances across film and television. He gained widespread recognition for portraying Bruce Banner / Hulk in the Marvel Cinematic Universe and for his critically acclaimed roles in films such as "Spotlight" and "The Kids Are All Right". Ruffalo is also noted for his outspoken advocacy on environmental and social issues, including climate change and political reform. Over his career, he has received multiple awards and nominations, including an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor.

Early life and education

Born and raised in Springfield, Ohio, Ruffalo developed an interest in acting during high school. He attended the University of Wisconsin–Madison, where he studied theater and theater arts. After completing his education, he moved to New York City to pursue acting professionally, working in theater productions and small film roles before gaining wider recognition.

Career

Ruffalo began his acting career in the early 1990s with appearances in various television series and independent films. He gained breakthrough with his performances in independent films, which led to supporting roles in Hollywood productions. His role as the Hulk in the Marvel Cinematic Universe starting with "The Avengers" (2012) significantly elevated his fame. He has also starred in films like "Shutter Island", "Foxcatcher", and "I Know This Much Is True", demonstrating his range as an actor. Beyond acting, Ruffalo is involved in producing and activism, founding organizations focused on environmental causes and social justice.

Notable works and projects

Ruffalo's most prominent role has been as Bruce Banner / Hulk in the Marvel franchise, appearing in multiple films including "The Avengers", "Age of Ultron", "Infinity War", and "Endgame". His performance in the drama "Spotlight" (2015) earned him several critical praises and awards. He also starred in "The Kids Are All Right" (2010), which earned him an Oscar nomination, and "Dark Waters" (2019), a legal thriller addressing environmental pollution. As an executive producer, he has been involved in documentary projects that highlight social issues.

Personal life

Ruffalo has been married to actress Sunrise Coigney since 2000, and they have three children. He is known for his advocacy on environmental issues, supporting renewable energy and climate change initiatives. Ruffalo is also outspoken about political reform, civil rights, and anti-corruption efforts, often using his platform to raise awareness and promote activism.

Awards and honours

Throughout his career, Ruffalo has received numerous awards and nominations, including a Screen Actors Guild Award, Critics' Choice Movie Award, and Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor. He has also received recognition for his activism and contributions to social justice causes, including awards from environmental organizations.

Legacy and impact

Recognized for his acting talent and advocacy, Ruffalo has become a prominent figure in Hollywood and beyond. His performances are praised for their depth and authenticity, and his activism has inspired many in the entertainment industry and the public to engage with environmental and social issues. His work continues to influence both film artistry and social consciousness.
