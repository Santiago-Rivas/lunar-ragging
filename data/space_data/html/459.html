Jacqueline Ann Novogratz is an American social entrepreneur, author, and founder of Acumen, an organization dedicated to tackling global poverty through entrepreneurial approaches. Recognized for her innovative work in impact investing and social change, she has become a prominent voice in philanthropy and international development.

Early life and education

Born in Buffalo, New York, Novogratz was raised in a family that valued community service and social consciousness. She studied in the United States and completed her undergraduate studies at the University of Virginia. She earned an MBA from Harvard Business School, where she developed a deep interest in development and social innovation.

Career

Novogratz began her career in finance, working in emerging markets before transitioning into the nonprofit sector in the 1990s. In 2001, she founded Acumen Fund, a nonprofit impact investment fund that supports social enterprises addressing poverty in several countries. Under her leadership, the organization has invested in businesses providing affordable healthcare, clean energy, and education in impoverished communities. She has also authored several books and speaks globally on social entrepreneurship and change.

Notable works and projects

Her best‑known work, The Blue Sweater: Bridging the Gap Between Rich and Poor in an Interconnected World, explores her journey in understanding global poverty and the importance of innovative solutions. She has also been involved in various initiatives promoting social innovation, including partnerships with governments and private sector entities to develop scalable solutions for development challenges.

Personal life

Novogratz is known for her dedication to social justice and sustainability. She is married and has children. She frequently emphasizes the importance of empathy, humility, and collaboration in addressing complex global issues. Her personal experiences driving her work include a commitment to transforming philanthropic approaches to create sustainable impact.

Awards and honours

Jacqueline Novogratz has received numerous recognitions, including the Skoll Award for Social Entrepreneurship, the Margaret Mead Award, and recognition by Fast Company as one of the Most Creative People in Business. She is also a fellow at the Aspen Institute and a board member of several philanthropic organizations.

Legacy and impact

Her work has inspired a new paradigm in social investing and development, emphasizing the power of entrepreneurship to improve living standards. Novogratz's approach has influenced countless social entrepreneurs, investors, and policymakers dedicated to sustainable development. Her writings continue to influence thought leadership in the field of social innovation.
