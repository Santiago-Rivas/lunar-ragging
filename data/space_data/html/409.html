Amanda Gorman is an American poet and activist known for her powerful spoken-word poetry and compelling performances. Rising to prominence as the youngest inaugural poet in U.S. history, she gained international recognition for her poem "The Hill We Climb" at President Joe Biden’s inauguration in January 2021. Her evocative voice and lyrical talent have made her a prominent figure in contemporary poetry and social commentary.

Early life and education

Born and raised in Los Angeles, Amanda Gorman was diagnosed with a benign brain tumor as a child, which led to surgeries and ongoing medical challenges. She developed an early love of poetry, often writing to process her experiences. She attended Harvard University, where she graduated with honors in Sociology in 2020, and furthered her studies in creative writing and literature.

Career

Gorman began her poetic career in her teens, performing at local events and national competitions. Her first poetry collection, "The One for Whom Food Is Not Enough," was published in 2015. Her breakthrough came when she was selected to recite her poem at President Biden’s inauguration in 2021, which garnered widespread acclaim. She has since performed at major events, released a bestselling book, and appeared on television programs, advocating for social justice, diversity, and hope.

Notable works and projects

Her debut poetry collection, "The Hill We Climb" (2021), received critical praise and was selected as a bestseller. Gorman also authored "Call Us What We Obsess Over" (2023). She has collaborated with organizations like the Obama Foundation and has participated in initiatives promoting literacy and youth empowerment. Her work often addresses themes of equality, resilience, and collective healing, resonating with audiences worldwide.

Personal life

Amanda Gorman is known for her advocacy and inspiring messages. She is openly LGBTQ+, and her experiences with adversity have shaped her outlook and writing. She continues to speak publicly about social issues, mental health, and the importance of art in societal change. Gorman lives in Los Angeles and remains committed to amplifying marginalized voices through her poetry and activism.

Awards and honours

She has received numerous awards, including the 2021 National YoungArts Foundation Award, the 2021 NAACP Image Award, and the 2022 Grammy Award for Best Spoken Word Album for her reading of "The Hill We Climb." Gorman was also named one of TIME magazine’s 100 Most Influential People in 2021 and has received honorary degrees from several institutions. Her influence continues to grow as a leading voice among young poets and activists.

Legacy and impact

Amanda Gorman’s emergence as a prominent poet has inspired a new generation of writers and speakers. Her blend of activism and artistry underscores the power of poetry to foster social awareness and resilience. As both a symbol of hope and a advocate for justice, her work advocates for unity and emphasizes the transformative potential of art in challenging times.
