Christiana María Figueres Elsie is a Costa Rican diplomat known for her leadership in global climate change initiatives. She served as the Executive Secretary of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) from 2010 to 2016, playing a pivotal role in facilitating the adoption of the Paris Agreement. Widely recognized for her advocacy and diplomacy, she has been a prominent voice in international efforts to combat climate change and promote sustainable development.

Early life and education

Born in San José, Costa Rica, Figueres was raised in a family actively involved in politics and environmental issues. She studied at the University of Costa Rica, earning a degree in anthropology and later obtained a Ph.D. in social anthropology from the Autonomous University of Barcelona. Her academic background laid a foundation for her career in environmental diplomacy and international negotiations.

Career

Beginning her career in environmental policy in the 1980s, Figueres held various positions within Costa Rica’s government and international organizations. In 2010, she was appointed as the UNFCCC Executive Secretary, a role she held until 2016. During her tenure, she led negotiations that resulted in the historic Paris Agreement, bringing nearly 200 countries together to commit to significant climate actions. After leaving the UNFCCC, she continued her activism through advisory roles, speaking engagements, and establishing initiatives to accelerate climate action worldwide.

Notable works and projects

Figueres is known for championing the Paris Agreement and fostering global cooperation on climate change. She co-founded the Vision 2050 project with the Global Compact and the World Business Council for Sustainable Development, envisioning a sustainable future for humanity and the planet. She also authored "The Future We Choose," a book emphasizing the importance of individual and collective action to address climate challenges.

Personal life

Christiana Figueres is married and has children. She is known for her dedication to environmental causes, her resilience in diplomacy, and her ability to bring diverse stakeholders to consensus. She remains active in climate advocacy, participating in international forums, and mentoring new generations of leaders committed to sustainable development.

Awards and honours

Her contributions have earned her numerous awards, including the Global Leadership Award from the United Nations Foundation and recognition as one of the world's leading environmental figures. She has been featured on lists of influential women and climate champions, emphasizing her impact on global policy and awareness.

Legacy and impact

Figueres' leadership has significantly shaped global climate policy. Her emphasis on multilateral diplomacy, equity, and urgent action has influenced international agreements and national commitments. Her work continues to inspire policymakers, businesses, and individuals to advance climate solutions and foster a sustainable future for all.
