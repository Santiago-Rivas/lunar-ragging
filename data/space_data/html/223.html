Roberto Carlos da Silva Rocha, born on 10 April 1973, is a Brazilian former professional footballer renowned for his powerful shots, exceptional pace, and accurate free kicks. After a highly successful playing career primarily with Real Madrid, he transitioned into coaching, working with various clubs and national teams. Considered one of the greatest left-backs in football history, Roberto Carlos's influence extend beyond his defensive skills, with his offensive contributions and set-piece expertise earning him widespread acclaim.

Early life and education

Born in Garça, São Paulo, Roberto Carlos began playing football at a young age. Demonstrating prodigious talent, he joined Palmeiras's youth system at age 17 before making his senior debut. His early years showcased his exceptional dribbling, speed, and powerful shooting, traits that would define his career. Despite initially focusing on playing at the local level, his talent quickly drew attention from larger clubs in Brazil and Europe.

Career

Roberto Carlos's professional career started in Brazil with Palmeiras, where he debuted in 1990. His impressive performances led to a transfer to Inter Milan in 1995, but he soon moved to Real Madrid in 1996 for a then-record transfer fee. Over the next decade, he established himself as a key player for Los Blancos, winning numerous La Liga titles and three UEFA Champions League titles. Known for his iconic free kicks, he scored many crucial goals from set pieces. After leaving Madrid, he played for clubs including Fenerbahçe, Corinthians, and Anzhi Makhachkala before retiring in 2015. Post-retirement, he shifted focus to coaching, working with the Brazil national team, Russian clubs, and Rio de Janeiro-based youth teams.

Notable works and projects

Beyond his club success, Roberto Carlos participated in international tournaments with Brazil, winning the FIFA World Cup in 2002 and numerous Copa América titles. His free-kick against France in 1997 is widely regarded as one of the greatest in football history. Off the field, he has been involved in various charitable activities, notably promoting sports among youth and supporting football development programs in Brazil. He also featured in advertising campaigns and endorsed several products, leveraging his global sporting fame.

Personal life

Roberto Carlos is married to his wife, Mariana Criscuolo, and the couple has children together. Known for his humble personality, he maintains a low profile outside football. He holds dual citizenship in Brazil and Italy, motivated by his heritage and playing career abroad. Roberto Carlos is also an avid fan of music, especially samba and MPB, and enjoys practicing jiu-jitsu and other sports in his leisure time. His faith and family are central to his personal life.

Awards and honours

Throughout his career, Roberto Carlos received numerous accolades including the FIFA World Cup Best Young Player award (1998), and he was named in the FIFA World Cup All-Star Team in 2002. He was included several times in the UEFA Champions League Squad of the Season and received Brazil’s Order of Merit. His free-kick goals and assists earned him recognition as one of the best set-piece specialists of his era.

Legacy and impact

Roberto Carlos is widely regarded as one of the most influential left-backs in football history. His speed, defensive solidity, and offensive ability transformed the role of full-backs in modern football. Many players and coaches cite him as an inspiration for his powerful free kicks and work ethic. His success helped elevate the global profile of Brazilian football and inspired a generation of attacking-minded full-backs. Off the pitch, his work promoting sports and youth development continues to impact communities in Brazil and beyond.
