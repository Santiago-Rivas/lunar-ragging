Boyan Slat is a Dutch inventor and environmentalist known for pioneering efforts to remove plastic pollution from the oceans. At a young age, he conceptualized and launched The Ocean Cleanup, a project dedicated to developing advanced technologies to extract floating plastic debris from the world’s oceans. His innovative approach and advocacy have made him a prominent figure in the fight against marine pollution.

Early life and education

Born in Gorinchem, Netherlands, Slat developed an interest in sustainability and engineering during his childhood. Inspired by media reports on plastic pollution and the promise of technology to tackle environmental issues, he pursued studies in aerospace engineering at Delft University of Technology. While still a student, he founded The Ocean Cleanup in 2012, motivated by a desire to make a tangible impact on ocean health.

Career

Slat's initial concept for removing plastic from the oceans gained attention as a crowdfunding campaign and a viral TEDx talk in 2013. Over the years, he led the development of large-scale floating cleanup systems designed to passively collect plastic debris. The first operational systems were deployed in the North Pacific Gyre, where they successfully tested and refined the technology. His organization has expanded its scope, attempting to address plastic in rivers worldwide as a precursor to ocean deployment.

Notable works and projects

The Ocean Cleanup has developed several iterations of passive cleanup systems, including the Interceptor, a river-based plastic collection device. The organization has garnered global media attention and partnerships with governments and corporations to scale efforts. Slat has been a keynote speaker at numerous environmental and technological conferences, advocating for innovative engineering solutions to ecological challenges. His team continues to improve the efficiency and scope of ocean cleanup systems, aiming to clean up a significant portion of ocean plastic by 2040.

Personal life

Boyan Slat maintains a relatively private personal life. He resides in the Netherlands and is committed to environmental causes. An avid supporter of technological innovation and sustainability, he invests personal time in developing new methods for waste collection and ocean conservation. He has expressed motivation from childhood experiences and a deep concern for future generations’ environmental health.

Awards and honours

Slat has received numerous awards recognizing his innovation and activism, including the Edison Award in 2016, the Swiss Sustainability Award in 2018, and recognition from the United Nations Environment Programme. His efforts have also earned him a place on Forbes’ 30 Under 30 list in the category of Enterprise Technology. He continues to inspire young entrepreneurs and environmentalists worldwide.

Legacy and impact

Boyan Slat’s work has significantly increased global awareness of ocean plastic pollution and has proven the potential of engineering and innovation to address environmental problems at scale. His project's technological advancements inspire future sustainable engineering solutions and policies aimed at reducing plastic waste and protecting marine ecosystems. As a role model, he exemplifies how youthful ingenuity can lead to impactful environmental change.
