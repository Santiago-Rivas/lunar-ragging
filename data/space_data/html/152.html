William Bradley Pitt, born on 18 December 1963 in Omaha, Nebraska, is an acclaimed American actor and film producer. He rose to international fame with roles in films such as Fight Club, Se7en, and Ocean's Eleven. Known for his versatility and charismatic screen presence, Pitt has received multiple accolades, including an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for his role in Once Upon a Time in Hollywood. Beyond acting, he has established a successful production company and actively supports humanitarian causes.

Early life and education

Pitt was raised in Springfield, Missouri, the only child of a family with a background in education and entrepreneurship. He attended the University of Missouri, where he studied journalism before relocating to Los Angeles in the late 1980s to pursue an acting career. His early years included small television roles and auditioning for various parts, gradually building his portfolio through guest spots and commercials.

Career

Brad Pitt's breakthrough came in 1991 with the film Thelma & Louise, which established him as a rising star. Throughout the 1990s, he became a leading man in several successful films, such as Legends of the Fall and Interview with the Vampire. The 2000s marked his collaboration with prominent directors like Quentin Tarantino and David Fincher, delivering critically acclaimed performances in movies like Fight Club and The Curious Case of Benjamin Button. Aside from acting, he co-founded Plan B Entertainment, producing several award-winning films including 12 Years a Slave and Moonlight. In recent years, Pitt received widespread praise for his role in Once Upon a Time in Hollywood, earning him the Academy Award and Golden Globe for Best Supporting Actor.

Notable works and projects

In addition to his acting roles, Pitt has been involved in numerous philanthropic efforts, particularly through the Make It Right Foundation and his work on rebuilding New Orleans after Hurricane Katrina. His production company, Plan B, has produced critical and commercial successes, including the films World War Z and Killing Them Softly. He has also directed several short films, showcasing his versatility behind the camera. Off-screen, Pitt is known for his advocacy on environmental issues, global health, and refugee support.

Personal life

Pitt has been married twice, first to actress Jennifer Aniston and later to actress Angelina Jolie, with whom he has six children. He maintains a relatively private personal life, often focusing on his family and philanthropy. Pitt has faced public scrutiny related to his relationships and legal efforts involving his children but remains a prominent figure in Hollywood and global humanitarian circles.

Awards and honours

Over his career, Pitt has received numerous awards, including an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor, three Golden Globe Awards, and various Screen Actors Guild awards. He was named People magazine’s Sexiest Man Alive in 1995 and has been recognized as one of the world’s most influential people by Time magazine multiple times. His contributions extend beyond entertainment, receiving humanitarian accolades for his philanthropic work.

Legacy and impact

Brad Pitt is regarded as one of Hollywood’s leading actors, admired for his acting range and commitment to social causes. His influence can be seen in his ability to choose diverse roles that challenge genre conventions, inspiring a new generation of actors. As a producer, he has helped bring critically acclaimed projects to the screen and fostered new talent. His honorary recognitions reflect his enduring impact on film and society, solidifying his status as an entertainment icon and humanitarian.
