Masayoshi Son is a Japanese billionaire entrepreneur and investor known for founding SoftBank Group Corp., a multinational conglomerate with a focus on technology, telecommunications, and investment. He is recognized for his aggressive investment strategies in technology startups worldwide and for shaping the telecommunications industry in Japan through his leadership of SoftBank. Over the decades, he has become one of Japan's most influential business figures, guiding the company through significant acquisitions and investments, including notable stakes in global technology firms.

Early life and education

Born in Tokuyama (now Shunan), Yamaguchi Prefecture, Son was raised in a family that emigrated from Korea. Demonstrating early entrepreneurial spirit, he moved to the United States at the age of 16 to attend the University of California, Berkeley, where he studied economics. During his time in the U.S., he cultivated a passion for technology and entrepreneurship, founding his first startup at age 19. After completing his degree in 1980, he returned to Japan to pursue his business ambitions.

Career

Masayoshi Son founded SoftBank in 1981 initially as a software distributor, but he shifted focus towards Internet and telecommunications businesses as the sector developed. His strategic foresight led SoftBank to become a major player in the mobile industry in Japan through the acquisition and development of telecom assets, including SoftBank Mobile. Son gained international prominence through early investments in tech companies such as Alibaba, Yahoo!, and Sprint. Under his leadership, SoftBank launched the Vision Fund—a massive technology investment fund capable of making multibillion-dollar investments in startup companies globally.

Notable works and projects

Son’s notable achievements include transforming SoftBank into a global technology investment powerhouse and facilitating the expansion of mobile telecommunications in Japan. His Vision Fund has invested in numerous high-profile startups, including Uber, WeWork, and Arm Holdings, influencing the global tech landscape. He has championed the integration of AI, IoT, and cloud computing into his company's operations and investments. Son is also involved in initiatives supporting renewable energy and advanced robotics.

Personal life

Son is known for being intensely private about his personal affairs. He is married and has children. His interests include classical music, historical biographies, and collecting rare art pieces. Despite his wealth, he remains grounded in his entrepreneurial pursuits and emphasizes the importance of technology in societal advancement. Son has expressed his vision of a future driven by AI, renewable energy, and connectivity, often inspiring discussions around innovation and the role of enterprise in shaping the world.

Awards and honours

Son has received numerous recognitions for his contributions to business and technology, including Japan’s Order of the Rising Sun and various industry awards. He has been featured on Forbes’ list of the world’s richest individuals and is renowned for his influence in the technology sector. His foresight and investment acumen have earned him praise from peers and analysts worldwide.

Legacy and impact

Masayoshi Son’s leadership helped revolutionize Japan’s telecommunications industry and positioned SoftBank as a global investor in technology startups. His approach to risk and investment has influenced a new generation of entrepreneurs and investors. Through his vision fund and strategic investments, he has accelerated innovation in AI, robotics, and telecommunications, shaping modern technological progress and fostering international collaboration in the tech industry.
