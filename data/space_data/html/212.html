Christine Margaret Sinclair (born 12 June 1983) is a renowned Canadian professional soccer player and captain of the Canadian women’s national team. Known for her exceptional goal-scoring ability, leadership, and longevity in international football, Sinclair has become one of the most accomplished players in the history of the sport. With over 300 international goals, she is the all-time leading goal scorer among male and female players in senior international soccer, shaping Canada’s reputation on the global stage for more than two decades.

Early life and education

Born and raised in Burnaby, British Columbia, Sinclair developed an early passion for soccer. She played competitive youth soccer from a young age, demonstrating talent and determination that set her apart. She attended Seaquam Secondary School, where she balanced academics and athletics, and her impressive performances at the youth level caught the attention of national selectors, paving the way for her international debut as a teenager.

Career

Sinclair debuted for the Canadian national team in 2000 at the age of 16. Over her extensive career, she has participated in multiple FIFA Women's World Cups, Olympic Games, Pan American Games, and CONCACAF tournaments. Her scoring prowess has helped Canada reach significant milestones, including a historic bronze medal at the 2012 London Olympics—the country’s first Olympic medal in women’s soccer. She has also played professionally for clubs in Sweden, Australia, and the United States, contributing to the growth of women’s club soccer through her skill and leadership.

Notable works and projects

Beyond her on-field achievements, Sinclair has been a trailblazer for women in sports, advocating for gender equality and increased investment in women’s football. She has served as a role model for young athletes across Canada and worldwide. Her involvement in community initiatives, youth development programs, and campaigns for equality have further cemented her legacy as a sports icon. She was named to the Order of Canada in recognition of her contributions to the sport and her country.

Personal life

Sinclair is known for her leadership, humility, and commitment to community service. She is married to fellow athlete and coach Corey Markham, and the couple has children. Off the field, she enjoys spending time with family, supporting youth sports, and advocating for social causes. Her dedication to her family and community underscores her status as a role model for integrity and perseverance.

Awards and honours

Throughout her career, Sinclair has received numerous individual awards, including the Vancouver Sun Young Athlete of the Year (2000), the Canadian Player of the Year multiple times, and was a finalist for the FIFA Women’s World Player of the Year. Her achievements include being the top scorer at two FIFA Women’s World Cups and a multiple-time winner of the CONCACAF Women’s Championship. In recognition, she has received honors such as the Order of British Columbia and FIFA Centennial Award for Excellence.

Legacy and impact

Sinclair’s impact extends beyond her goal-scoring records; she has been instrumental in elevating the profile of women’s soccer in Canada and internationally. Her leadership on and off the pitch has inspired a new generation of female athletes and contributed to the development of professional women’s leagues. Her career exemplifies dedication, resilience, and the power of sport to promote social change, leaving a lasting legacy in Canadian and global football.
