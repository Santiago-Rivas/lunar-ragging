Reshma Saujani is an American lawyer, politician, and author known for founding the nonprofit organization Girls Who Code and for her advocacy promoting women in technology and leadership roles. She has been a prominent voice in efforts to close the gender gap in STEM fields and encourage young women to pursue careers in tech and entrepreneurship. Her work emphasizes the importance of resilience, courage, and community support in overcoming barriers faced by women and marginalized groups in various sectors.

Early life and education

Born and raised in Kansas City, Missouri, Saujani earned her undergraduate degree from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. She subsequently obtained a Juris Doctor degree from Yale Law School. During her early career, she worked as a lawyer and in public service, focusing on issues related to social justice and community development.

Career

After practicing law and holding positions in government, Saujani launched her political career, running unsuccessfully for Congress in 2010. Following her defeat, she dedicated herself to addressing gender disparities in technology, founding Girls Who Code in 2012. The organization seeks to equip young women with coding skills and confidence to pursue careers in tech. Saujani has also authored books and delivered talks emphasizing the importance of bravery and perseverance in professional and personal growth.

Notable works and projects

Saujani's book "Women Who Don't Wait in Line" (2013) explores challenges women face in ambition and leadership, advocating for proactive risk-taking. Her organization, Girls Who Code, has expanded externally to include multiple programs nationwide, inspiring thousands of girls to learn coding. She also co-founded Makela, a startup dedicated to mentorship and skill-building for women entrepreneurs. Her public speaking engagements often center on fostering diverse leadership and combating systemic biases in industries like technology and politics.

Personal life

Saujani is married and has children. She is known for her advocacy for personal resilience and community involvement. She lives in New York City, balancing her professional pursuits with her family life. Her personal experiences with cultural identity and perseverance influence her leadership style and activism.

Awards and honours

Saujani has received multiple recognitions, including being named to Forbes' "Most Powerful Women" list and Fast Company's "Most Creative People in Business." She has also been recognized by the Clinton Foundation and was a TED speaker, sharing her message of courage and community empowerment. Her work has garnered awards for social innovation and educational impact.

Legacy and impact

Reshma Saujani has played a pivotal role in inspiring young women to pursue careers in technology and leadership. Her advocacy has contributed to increased awareness of gender disparities and has driven policy and educational initiatives to promote diversity in STEM fields. Her emphasis on bravery over perfection has resonated with many emerging entrepreneurs and activists, positioning her as a leading figure in the modern movement for gender equality and social change.
