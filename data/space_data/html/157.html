Anne Jacqueline Hathaway (born 12 November 1982) is an American actress and singer known for her versatility and performances across film genres. She gained fame with her role as Princess Mia Thermopolis in "The Princess Diaries" (2001), which established her as a rising star. Hathaway received widespread acclaim for her portrayal of Fantine in "Les Misérables" (2012), earning the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress. Her career spans over two decades, during which she has starred in critically acclaimed films, including "The Devil Wears Prada," "Rachel Getting Married," and "Interstellar," showcasing her range from comedy to intense drama.

Early life and education

Born and raised in Brooklyn, New York City, Hathaway was initially encouraged to pursue acting at a young age. She attended LaGuardia High School of Music & Art and Performing Arts and later studied at Vassar College, where she balanced her education with early acting opportunities. Her passion for performance was evident from her youth, leading to her debut in television and subsequent film roles as a teenager.

Career

Hathaway's breakthrough came with "The Princess Diaries" (2001), which made her a household name. She then demonstrated her acting chops in diverse roles, earning critical praise and multiple award nominations. Her transformation into Fantine in "Les Misérables" (2012) earned her an Academy Award, Golden Globe, and BAFTA. She continued to work in both mainstream blockbusters and independent films, often portraying complex characters. Hathaway has also been involved in producing films and is recognized for her advocacy in social and environmental causes.

Notable works and projects

In addition to her acting, Hathaway lent her voice to the animated film "Ratatouille" (2007). She played the White Queen in "Alice in Wonderland" (2010) and starred in "Interstellar" (2014), directed by Christopher Nolan. She portrayed Catwoman in "The Dark Knight Rises" (2012), earning praise for her dynamic performance. Hathaway founded the production company "Hathaway & Co." and has been active in philanthropic initiatives, supporting gender equality, education, and climate change awareness.

Personal life

Hathaway married actor Adam Shulman in 2012, and the couple has two children. Known for her elegance and down-to-earth demeanor, she is also committed to charitable work, including the Dream Foundation and Step Up Women's Network. Hathaway is an advocate for environmental issues and has spoken about balancing her career with her family life. She resides primarily in Los Angeles with her family.

Awards and honours

Hathaway’s awards include an Academy Award, Golden Globe Award, Screen Actors Guild Award, and several critics' choice recognitions. She has been nominated for multiple awards for her performances in films like "Rachel Getting Married," "Les Misérables," and "Interstellar." She was named one of Time magazine’s 100 most influential people in the world in 2013 and has received various humanitarian awards for her advocacy work.

Legacy and impact

Anne Hathaway is regarded as one of the most talented actresses of her generation, celebrated for her ability to embody diverse characters and bring authenticity to her roles. She has influenced aspiring performers and contributed to a broader conversations about gender equality and social responsibility in Hollywood. Her philanthropic efforts have heightened awareness around important social issues, reaffirming her role as a positive influence both on and off-screen.
